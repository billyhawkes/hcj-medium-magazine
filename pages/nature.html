Sea-levels rise to new heights.

By Billy Hawkes

·

March 26

Levels highest in over 2,000 years

The rates at which sea levels are rising keep growing. A main proponent of this is climate change, where as the world temperature rises, this causes oceans to melt. In just the last 100 years, we have increased more than double that of the 2000 years before.

Sea level charts show the main culprets

Researchers call these charts, "sea-level budgets". These budgets help quantify the rise in sea level over time. They are sectioned based on coutry, region and locally. All sections have different types of effects. Regional can be attributed to temperature and melting ice, whereas locally, it can be things like water collection and land subsidence, the release of water from certain types of rocks.

Climate Change increasing the rising rate

In nature, there is a natural trend of rising an lowering sea levels. But in the last few centuries scientists can see a more abnormal trend. Also with the increase in global storming events such as the Australian flooding, these play a role in the rise.
